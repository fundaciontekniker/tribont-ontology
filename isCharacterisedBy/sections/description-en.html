isCharacterisedBy ontology design pattern : Description back to ToC

The goal of this ontology design pattern is to characterise a subject or group of subjects of interest (SubjectOfInterest class) by assigning qualifiable or quantifiable attributes or characteristics (Property class). Quantitative properties can be described either by a specific value (value data property) or a range of values (minValue, masValue data properties), and including the unit of measurement (Unit class and hasUnit object property). A property cannot exist without characterising at least a subject of interest.

As described a SubjectOfInterest class can describe either a subject of interest or a grouping of subjects of interest which have similar characteristics. If it represents an aggrupation, the subjects of interest belonging to the group inherits all the properties that characterise it and can be further particularised.

Competency Questions

This section shows the specification of the requirements for the isCharacterisedBy design pattern in the form of key basic Competency Questions..

  • CQ.01: What are the properties that characterise a subject of interest?
  • CQ.02: What are the subjects of interest belonging to a given subjects of interest?
  • CQ.03: Which are the values and unit of measurement of a property?
